Vesicode's role in extracellular vesicle research

At Vesicode, we are dedicated to providing exceptional products and services for the analysis and profiling of surface membrane proteins of extracellular vesicles (EVs). Our innovative EV characterization panels makes use of our Proximity Barcoding Assay (PBA) for accurate proteomic profiling.

Our advanced multiplex immunoassay allows researchers to detect, quantify and stratify individual extracellular vesicles with minimal sample consumption.

Is this applicable to my research?

Yes, and this is why

The composition of EVs in biological samples may confer valuable information. However, despite the recent development of new technologies for EV detection, the analytical methods employed are limited in their accessibility of such valuable information. Thus, there is a great need for novel methods for analysis of EVs.

Our patented PBA technology allows for a high throughput approach for simultaneous profiling of 100+ surface proteins present on EVs, using minute sample volumes. Fully validated in serum and plasma, PBA is also compatible with a myriad of different sample types such as cell lysates, and purifed or unpurified EVs among other.

Performed on a solid support, our PBA analysis requires no compartmentalization of samples in nanowells, droplets and no additional sample preparation, which simplifies and streamlines the overall detection process. PBA uses specific tags in order to identify and quantify larger numbers of protein combinations present on the surface of the EVs as compared to most currently applied methods.

Thus, offering our customers a truly unique way of analyzing EVs.

Our Proximity Barcoding Assay technology

The innovative method used in our extracellular vesicle surface protein detection panel allows for a high throughput, multiplex assay that simultaneously profiles over 100 surface proteins on individual vesicles using 1-2 microliter of serum, plasma, or other biological sample types.

Biomarker discovery at new dimensions

Our ability for multiplex analysis of individual EVs in small samples, confers a new dimension to biomarker discovery methods. Through the use of the high-multiplex PBA method we are able to better accurately detect, quantify, and stratify these novel biomarker molecules based on the multivariate analysis of protein patterns present. This enables for a better insight in to the roles of extracellular vesicles in biological processes, and disease detection and processes, which enables the use of EVs as potential biomarkers.

Vesicode offers today, an ever expanding range of profiling panels that provides a new dimension for biomarker discovery.
